Visual concerns related to color

5

Sept. 2023

We live in a world surrounded by such vibrant and treasurable things that we may sometimes neglect to take advantage of. What if the way you saw the world wasn't the exact way others saw it because of a color-based impairment or disability? Amblyopia, Cataract, Glaucoma, topic nerve atrophy and more are a few examples of visual concerns that can be aided with web accessibility. A more extensive list can be found on Stroggo.com.

 

One color-based impairment is color blindness, with red/green being the most popular type. Color blindness (or monochromacy) occurs when the anatomy of someone's eyes don't allow them to see the world as normal eyes do. There are three common types: red-green, blue-yellow and complete color blindness (Lera, par. 4, 2021). To aid in an impairment or disability like this, web developers would have to not only consider color choice but also the functionality needed for screen readers to understand the information accurately to colorblind users. Color blindness isn't as rare as people may assume, “there are an estimate of “approximately 300 million people across the globe” impacted by this eye condition. What causes this condition is due to the “three types of cone cells that are able to discern different colors and shades” (Lera, par. 2, 2021).

 

A few important accessibility topics to keep in mind from the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) include: “Contrast (Minimum)”, “Use of Color” and “Non-Text Content” (What Is Color Blindness Accessibility?, pars. 8-12, 2022). To assist color blind users as a web developer, it's important to: avoid using red and green for status color or interactive elements, to emphasize ratio of a color contrast ratio of at least 4.5:1 (Web accessibility guidelines v1.0 foundations - overview, par. 1, 2023). For our website we know that color contrast is an important topic and there are a few resources to use to help us with this: Stark, Adobe Color, and Contrast (Levanier, par. 26, 2021).

 

Another colorblind friendly idea for the development on our website will be to use textures in conjunction with color to remove the uncertainty of color concerns.
